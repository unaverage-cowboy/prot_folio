About Me

My name is Victor, and I'm a Cognitive Scientist (in the making). I reside in the San Francisco Bay area and am an undergrad at The University of California, Berkeley.

Over time I have accumulated diverse life experiences and a critical approach to seeing the world around me. In contrast, academia has taught me to be analytical, evidence-based, and data-oriented. I incorporate both aspects into my life philosophy.

My goal is to make 'things' approachable and accessible. Where by things, I mean knowledge and design. I believe knowledge should be understandable and helpful, and products should be designed to be intuitive and usable by their intended audience. This is possible with science, empathy, a sprinkle of a design eye, and me.

I'm a lab assistant for the Whitney Lab for Perception and Action at UC Berkeley. In the future, I want to continue pursuing research in Emotion in Visual Perception and its applications to Human-Computer Interaction.

A few of my hobbies include eating lemon pie, posing inane questions, passing docstrings, film photography, and good cardio on the hills.
