My name is Victor, and I'm a Cognitive Scientist (in the making). I reside in the San Francisco Bay area, and I'm currently an undergrad student at The University of California, Berkeley. A glorified trade school where the intellectual discourse is merely performative.

Over time I have accumulated diverse life experiences and a critical approach to seeing the world around me, whereas academia has taught me to be analytical, evidence-based, and data-oriented. I incorporate both aspects into my life philosophy.

In an era where reality has become detached from objectivity, understanding our ontological position becomes necessary amidst the disruption. Artificial intelligence poses new challenges as to what it means to be human while decoding our brain's black box makes us question the degree to which we can control our decisions as individuals. Under this context, current social challenges are dependent on the information's flow.

My goal is to incorporate scientific knowledge into the mainstream and public sector areas. I believe science is flawed, yet it is the best methodology we have depended upon. Moreover, citizens can benefit from education through science projects, and most importantly, from data transparency. I aim to work with entities whose mission is to improve the human condition through science, education, and ethics—questioning their existing values through a humanistic approach.
